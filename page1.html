electromagnetic poles display
software development
As part of my engineering curriculum, I developed a sophisticated Java-based software catering to the academic community at the European School of Materials Science. Our collaborative team of three embraced the Agile methodology, with me as the Scrum Master. Direct collaboration with research faculty enabled us to meticulously outline requisites and expectations.
Leveraging Maxwell's equations and linear algebra, our software thrives on meticulous algorithmic underpinnings. Crafted with object-oriented Java and originally designed for dual charges, it evolved to accommodate infinite charge placements, adding versatility to its application.
Through a seamless user experience, charges are positioned and calibrated with ease through a dedicated configuration panel. Equipotential, potential's heat map and field lines are displayed. This user-friendly interface empowers scholars to refine charge values and positions, facilitating a nuanced study of electromagnetic behaviors.

Regular validation sessions with our researcher clients ensured that our progress aligned seamlessly with academic needs. Our software's ergonomic and accessible design reflects an unwavering commitment to both usability and inclusivity.
